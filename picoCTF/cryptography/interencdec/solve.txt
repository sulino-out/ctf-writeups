You get an file called enc_flag, which is double base64 encoded, so you just need to use the command:

$ cat enc_flag | base64 -d
b'd3BqdkpBTXtqaGx6aHlfazNqeTl3YTNrX2kyMDRoa2o2fQ=='

Take the other base64 encoded text inside the b'' and decode it again

$ echo "d3BqdkpBTXtqaGx6aHlfazNqeTl3YTNrX2kyMDRoa2o2fQ==" | base64 -d
wpjvJAM{jhlzhy_k3jy9wa3k_i204hkj6}

The output will be the flag encrypted with caesar cipher, decrypting it using dcode.fr, will give us the real flag:

picoCTF{caesar_d3cr9pt3d_b204adc6}
